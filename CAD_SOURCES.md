## Trade and education references

- [CAP Art et techniques de la bijouterie-joaillerie — RNCP36336](https://www.francecompetences.fr/recherche/rncp/36336/) — drawing conformity, thickness, alignment, stone/support compatibility, assembly, and finish.
- [GIA Bench Tip 17](https://www.gia.edu/articles/bench-tip-avoid-stone-loss-with-quality-assurance-benchmarks) — 70–80° prongs, 40–50% finished seat removal, crown contact, and finished height.
- [GIA — Design With Melee](https://www.gia.edu/design-with-melee) — melee selection and structural pavé borders.
- [Stuller CAD/CAM Production Standards](https://assets.stullercloud.com/web/apps/images/kbpdfs/stu7283-cadcamproductionstandards.pdf) — 0.45 mm prongs, 0.40 mm gallery details, 0.15 mm pavé spacing, and 0.50 mm culet/rail clearance for the selected production profile.
- [Morris & Watson — CAD Design Guidelines](https://www.morrisandwatson.com/casting/cad-design-guidelines) — direct-casting shrinkage, minimum details, openings, and finishing loss.
- [ISO 8653:2016](https://www.iso.org/standard/65408.html) — ring-size definition, measurement, and designation. Only the public summary was consulted.

## How rules are used

The engine classifies every check as one of:

- universal geometric or gemmological principle;
- finished-piece quality criterion;
- supplier/foundry production profile;
- configurable workshop threshold.

Supplier-specific values are not presented as universal CAP rules. Ring-body minima remain workshop warnings because no single value is valid for every alloy, section, process, and use.

## Guarantee boundary

The generated report is a pre-CAD geometric check. It cannot inspect porosity, previous solder joints, heat treatment, inclusions, stone treatment, work hardening, casting quality, setter pressure, polish loss, or final comfort. Final approval by the jeweller, setter, and foundry is mandatory.
